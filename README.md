## 🧩 Business Problem

> *"The retail business is struggling to understand WHY customers buy, WHO their most valuable customers are, and WHAT drives repeat purchases — leading to poor targeting, high churn, and missed revenue opportunities."*

The management team needed clear answers to these critical questions:

- Who are our highest-value customer segments by age, gender, and location?
- Which product categories generate the most revenue and repeat purchases?
- What payment methods and purchase frequencies are most common?
- How do seasonal trends affect customer buying behavior?
- Which customers are at risk of churn, and how can we retain them?
- What discount and promo strategies are actually working?

**Without these answers**, the business was making marketing and inventory decisions based on guesswork — wasting budget and losing customers.

## 🔍 My Workflow — 6 Professional Steps

### ✅ Step 1 — Data Preparation & EDA (Python)

**Tool:** Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn)

**What I did:**
- Imported the raw CSV dataset with 3,900+ customer records
- Checked and handled missing values, duplicate rows, and data type issues
- Standardized inconsistent column values (e.g., category labels, gender entries)
- Performed full Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):
  - Distribution of customer age, gender, and location
  - Purchase amount trends across categories
  - Correlation between discount usage and purchase value
  - Seasonal purchase pattern visualization

---

### ✅ Step 2 — Business Analysis (SQL)

**Tool:** SQL Server (MS SQL Server / MySQL / PostgreSQL)

**What I did:** Wrote targeted SQL queries to answer every business question:

- **Customer Segmentation** — grouped customers by age band, gender, and location to find the most profitable segments
- **Category Performance** — ranked product categories by total revenue and purchase frequency
- **Loyalty Analysis** — identified subscription vs non-subscription customers and their spending difference
- **Discount Effectiveness** — measured whether promo codes actually increased average order value
- **Seasonal Trends** — calculated monthly/seasonal revenue to find peak buying periods
- **Churn Risk** — flagged low-frequency customers with declining purchase amounts

## 💡 Key Business Insights & Outcomes

### 👥 Customer Segments
- **Male customers aged 25–45** account for the highest average purchase value
- **Subscription members** spend significantly more per order than non-subscribers, validating the loyalty program
- Montana, California, and Idaho emerged as the **top-revenue states**

### 🛍️ Product & Category Insights
- **Clothing and Accessories** are the highest revenue-generating categories
- **Footwear** shows the highest purchase frequency — customers come back more often
- **Outerwear** has the highest average order value but lowest frequency — a premium segment

### 📅 Seasonal Trends
- **Fall (Q4)** is the peak season — highest revenue and purchase volume
- **Spring** shows the sharpest drop — a clear opportunity for targeted seasonal promotions
- Businesses should plan inventory and marketing spend around Q4 spikes

### 💳 Payment & Discount Behavior
- **Credit Card** is the most popular payment method, followed by PayPal
- Customers using **promo codes spend 18–22% more** per transaction — confirming that discounts do drive higher order values
- **Free shipping** is a stronger purchase trigger than percentage discounts

### ⭐ Customer Satisfaction
- Average rating of **3.7 / 5** — room for improvement in product quality and delivery experience
- **Accessories** have the highest satisfaction scores; **Outerwear** has the lowest

## 🚀 How to Run This Project

### Step 1 — Python (Data Cleaning & EDA)
```bash
pip install pandas matplotlib seaborn sqlalchemy jupyter
jupyter notebook notebooks/Customer_Shopping_Behavior_Analysis.ipynb
```

### Step 2 — SQL (Business Analysis)
1. Open **SQL Server Management Studio** or **MySQL Workbench**
2. Create a new database: `customer_shopping_db`
3. Load the CSV via Python notebook or import wizard
4. Run queries from `sql/customer_behavior_sql_queries.sql`

### Step 3 — Power BI (Dashboard)
1. Open `powerbi/customer_behavior_dashboard.pbix` in **Power BI Desktop**
2. Update the data source connection to your SQL Server
3. Click **Refresh** to load the latest data
4. Explore with slicers: Gender, Season, Category, Subscription Status

### Step 4 — Report & Presentation
- Open `report/Customer_Shopping_Behavior_Analysis.pdf` to read the full findings
- Open `presentation/Customer-Shopping-Behavior-Analysis.pptx` for the deck
